About Ghana

Ghana, located on the Gulf of Guinea in West Africa, is celebrated for its stability, hospitality, and cultural vibrancy. From the historic Ashanti Kingdom to modern tech hubs in Accra, Ghana blends tradition and innovation.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Accra
  • Official Language: English (with many local languages including Akan, Ewe, Ga, Dagbani)
  • Currency: Ghana Cedi (GHS)
  • Independence: 6 March 1957
  • Flag: Red, Gold, Green with a Black Star

Culture

Colourful kente cloth, highlife and afrobeat music, energetic festivals, and delicious cuisine shape Ghana’s cultural identity. Community, respect, and warmth—Akwaaba—define the Ghanaian spirit.

Why Visit?

  1. Powerful heritage sites along the coast
  2. Eco‑tourism from rainforests to savannah
  3. Friendly people and vibrant arts scene
